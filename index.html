About

Research meets
production.

I build things at the intersection of AI research and real systems. I architected a multi-agent AI platform from scratch — LangGraph orchestration, 40+ MCP-federated tools, dual-layer RAG — and shipped it to staging in under four months. Then I stuck around to build the fulfillment backend powering the commerce layer underneath it. Because apparently I enjoy building the engine and the road.

On the research side, I work on computer vision for autonomous robots. My thesis fused CLIPSeg, OMDet, and SAMv2 for zero-shot object detection on mobile robots. I've published in IEEE, built navigation stacks validated in 40+ hours of field operation, and trained perception pipelines for quadruped robots. The quadruped didn't fall over, which I consider a personal achievement.

It all started with a Raspberry Pi duct-taped to an RC car for a college science fair. Camera on the hood, commands streaming to a laptop. Not elegant. But it drove itself — and that feeling of making something work that probably shouldn't have is still the thing that gets me out of bed.
